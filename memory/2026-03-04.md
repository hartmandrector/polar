## Riser Geometric Cell Pitch ‚Äî Force Vector Tilt (afternoon session)

### The Problem
Front risers turned canopy wrong direction. Œ± reduction + drag bump alone insufficient ‚Äî missing the primary turn mechanism: physical cell rotation tilting the entire force vector.

### Solution: `cellPitchRad` (commit `6a28ac6`)
- Added `cellPitchRad` to `getCoeffs()` return type and `SegmentForceResult`
- In `sumAllSegments()` and `evaluateAeroForcesDetailed()`: rotate force vector `(fx, fz)` by cellPitchRad in body x-z plane
- Front riser: positive pitch (nose down) ‚Üí lift tilts forward ‚Üí thrust on pulled side ‚Üí yaw toward input ‚úÖ
- Rear riser: negative pitch (nose up) ‚Üí lift tilts backward ‚Üí yaw toward input ‚úÖ
- `RISER_PITCH_MAX_RAD = 0.10` (~5.7¬∞) ‚Äî started at 0.08, tried 0.15 (too aggressive, caused AoA runaway), settled at 0.10
- Applied to both canopy cells and brake flaps

### Brake-to-Cell Coupling Reworked
- **Brake geometric cell pitch**: `BRAKE_PITCH_MAX_RAD = 0.06` (~3.4¬∞) nose up
- **Brake drag bump on parent cell**: `BRAKE_CD_BUMP = 0.08`
- **Center cell partial brake coupling**: `brakeInput = avg(L,R) * 0.5` (fabric tension)

### Brake Flap Polar Retuned
- `cl_alpha`: 4.0‚Üí2.0, `cd_0`: 0.02‚Üí0.06, `k`: 0.05‚Üí0.08
- `alpha_stall_fwd`: 70¬∞‚Üí18¬∞ (KEY ‚Äî Kirchhoff drag-dominated early)
- `s1_fwd`: 8‚Üí5 (sharper transition)

### Parent Cell Brake Derivatives Retuned

- Parent cell drag LOW under brakes ‚Äî flap handles drag, parent handles camber/lift
